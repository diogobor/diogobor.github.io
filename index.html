Profile

About & background

Holds a B.Sc. in Computer Science and an M.Sc. in Computer Science with a focus on Artificial Intelligence from the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ).

Completed a Ph.D. in Biosciences and Biotechnology at the Carlos Chagas Institute / Fiocruz, where a new search tool, SIM-XL, was developed for complex cross-linked peptide datasets in structural proteomics and protein–protein interaction studies.

Postdoctoral research at Institut Pasteur (Paris) focused on bioinformatics solutions for top-down proteomics, followed by work at Leibniz-FMP (Berlin) on cleavable cross-linkers and faster spectral search strategies. Currently a Research Scientist in computational proteomics at Exact Sciences.

Research

Research interests

Research spans bottom-up and top-down proteomics, quantitative mass spectrometry, structural mass spectrometry, and cross-linking strategies for studying protein complexes and protein–protein interactions.

Additional interests include algorithm design for proteomics search engines, spectrum clustering and visualization, and integrative workflows combining orthogonal MS strategies.

Selected themes

  • Search engines and pipelines for cross-linking MS (e.g., SIM-XL, XlinkCyNET).
  • Top-down proteomics tools and parameter optimization (e.g., TDFragMapper, ProteoCombiner).
  • Spectral clustering, quality control, and visualization (e.g., DiagnoTop, DiagnoMass).
  • Quantitative and multiplexed proteomics, including TMT-based XL-MS and isotope labeling.

Methods & algorithms

  • Natural optimization: genetic algorithms, particle swarm, ant colony, simulated annealing.
  • Statistical classifiers: SVMs, Bayesian methods, and related machine learning models.
  • Neural networks: feed-forward architectures with backpropagation for pattern recognition.
  • Statistical inference and experimental design for MS-based proteomics.
